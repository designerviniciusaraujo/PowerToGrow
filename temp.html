People and economies thrive when micro-enterprise are empowered to reach their full potential. Power to Grow: The Innovation Challenge is a search to discover and reward organizations that are empowering micro-enterprises in Central America.

If your organization is focused on increasing the capacity of Central American micro-enterprises to access regional and international markets, apply to Power to Grow by April 27th. Awardees will each receive a comprehensive support package, up to $43,000 to promote their solution, and partnership with a current member of La Red de Innovación e Impacto. Working together, the awardee and La Red member will leverage shared experience and knowledge, benefit from cross-sector collaboration, and forge a coalition to empower micro-enterprises.

Process

Selected candidates will be officially announced by May 31.

A committee of experts will be reviewing all the applications. Know that during this process, the candidates may be contacted in order to ask for additional information that might be required for final evaluation and selection.

Your proposed solution will be expected to begin by July, 2018 and last for a year to 15 months.

Up to 95% of companies in Central America are Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs). The success of these companies and the prosperity of communities are inextricably linked. However, many struggle to grow and remain competitive in the global marketplace. Power to Grow: The Innovation Challenge Fueled by La Red de Innovación e Impacto aims to support micro-enterprises by creating partnerships between mission-aligned organizations and current La Red members.

Accelerating innovation and job creation opportunities for Central American micro-enterprises in the agriculture, agro-industry, and textiles sectors;

Selection criteria for Incoming Class candidates, including characteristics to work collaboratively with Alumni members

    Common requirements:

  • Organization legally registered as a nonprofit. This includes NGOs, industry associations, cooperatives, foundations, academia, and other similar institutions;
  • Experience in industry sectors to be assisted (agriculture, agro-industry and/or textile);
  • A network of, or access to, micro-enterprise beneficiaries;
  • Experience in collaborations with private sector, NGOs, government and international entities providing assistance to cooperation programs, entrepreneurs, small farmers, small agriculture producers and/or SME’s (preferably micro and small enterprises);
  • Experience on scaling micro enterprises´ competitiveness, preferably related to improve exports;
  • Experience of more than five years on program execution in the areas being proposed (agriculture, agro-industry and/or textile);
  • Permanent presence in country to execute project activities with La Red Alumni, preferably with knowledge of the Central America region and international markets;
  • Capacity to manage, technical assistance for the framework of international cooperation programs;
  • Capacity to speak Spanish;
  • Available staff and organizational capacity to provide technical assistance and trainings.
